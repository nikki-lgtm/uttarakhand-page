About

T

he state is bordered by Himachal Pradesh to the northwest, Tibet to the north, Nepal to the east, Uttar Pradesh to the south and southeast, with a small part touching Haryana in the west. Uttarakhand has a total area of 53,483 km2 (20,650 sq mi), equal to 1.6% of the total area of India. Dehradun serves as the state capital, with Nainital being the judicial capital. The state is divided into two divisions, Garhwal and Kumaon, with a total of 13 districts. The forest cover in the state is 45.4% of the state's geographical area.The Ganges and its tributary Yamuna, originate from the Gangotri and Yamunotri glaciers respectively. Uttarakhand's name is derived from the words 'Uttar' meaning 'north', and 'khand' meaning 'section' or 'part', altogether simply meaning 'Northern Part'. The name finds mention in early Hindu scriptures as the combined region of "Kedarkhand","Manaskhand".

Instruments

B

hankhora is a traditional percussion instrument from Uttarakhand, played during folk music and cultural festivals. It produces deep, resonant beats and is often used in rituals and celebrations.

D

hol is a traditional percussion instrument of Uttarakhand, played during folk dances and festivals. It holds cultural significance, often used in rituals, celebrations, and spiritual ceremonies.

H

udka is a traditional instrument of Uttarakhand, resembling a small drum, played with a stick and hand. It is commonly used in folk music, especially during cultural and religious ceremonies.

T

urri is a traditional percussion instrument from Uttarakhand, played during folk rituals and festivals. It produces a loud, rhythmic sound, often accompanying dances and cultural performances.

M

Masak Been is a traditional wind instrument of Uttarakhand, resembling a bagpipe. It is commonly played by shepherds in the hills, producing soulful melodies.
